About Us

A Community
Built on Faith

Hammersmith Christian Fellowship was founded in 1901 as Bethel Baptist Church under the Pastorate of Rev. Alonzo Clark. We are proudly a member of the Old Baptist Union — a fellowship of evangelical Baptist churches committed to proclaiming the Word of God in its fullness.

We are a group of ordinary people discovering that faith in God is a life-changing experience. Committed by the Holy Spirit, we teach and live the gospel of our Lord Jesus Christ.

Our Mission

Serve God, serve each other, and serve the community.

Our Objective

Advance God's Kingdom through the Holy Spirit — equipping disciples.

Who We Are

Ordinary people discovering an extraordinary, life-changing faith.

All Welcome

Whatever your background — you are welcome here. Come as you are.

Our Faith

What We Believe

Our faith is grounded in the Word of God, the grace of Jesus Christ, and the fellowship of the Holy Spirit.

The Bible

We believe the Bible is the Word of God — the foundation of all our preaching and practice. The Gospel of Jesus Christ is exactly the good news our society needs to hear.

Believer's Baptism

We practise baptism by full immersion for those with personal faith in Jesus. By entering the waters, we proclaim the gospel — identifying with Christ's death, burial, and resurrection.

Communion

Communion is open to all who have a personal relationship with Jesus, regardless of church background — a fellowship of believers remembering the sacrifice of our Lord Jesus Christ.

Giving

Our finances are met by God through the regular giving of our members. An offering is taken during Sunday service — but visitors are never expected to contribute.

Our Story

Over 120 Years
of Faithful Service

Our story began in 1901 when Rev. Alonzo Clark founded a small fellowship in Greyhound Road, Fulham. Through wars, rebuilds, and renewal, HCF has remained a steadfast beacon of faith in West London.

1901
Founded by Rev. Alonzo Clark

Bethel Baptist Church established in Greyhound Road, Fulham — a member of the Old Baptist Union from its earliest days.

1937
Committed Lives & Wartime Witness

Rev. Les Clark committed his life to Christ at a New Year Watch Night service — a formative moment in the church's story. The fellowship held firm through the Blitz.

1956
A New Home on Dalling Road

In a remarkable step of faith, the church purchased the Methodist building on Dalling Road for £3,500 — against all odds — and moved in on June 2nd.

2000s
Renewal & Redevelopment

A bold vision transformed the building — the sanctuary moved upstairs, new facilities added below — making HCF fit for a new century of ministry.
